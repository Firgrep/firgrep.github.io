Outcry

Looking into the darkness of history
into actions and events as innumerable as indeterminate
the depth of automation before and beneath
a plane of ecstasy and excess.

Harrowing is this chamber of no windows
of no light that can pierce within
for only the eye that submits to its darkening ambiguity
will be enlightened by its cyclical certainty.

Generations upon generations in the oil of time
lie languishing prior to history
and prepare the emotions through their contest extreme
that later offspring may feel their tempered depth.

So far back that even death becomes technology
and jealousy an invention, envy a coinage;
but was there ever an age without the full armada of emotions
for we look back as those before us looked back
trying to find the cause outside its action
through motion rendered motionless
moments crystallized with suspended momentum
coiled to pierce through our astral wastes.

Amidst the worlds of this mausoleum
when the shades have all left their lamp posts
the body organ pipes through its chest all that these walls held
and returned its eternally echoing outcry.

Gold, Golden Is Your Brow

Gold, golden is your brow
When the clouds clear
And the land reaches out
To be comforted in your sunlit embrace,
The block of habit -
Is chiseled into feeling.

Long has the winter brought darkness,
Longest in memory yet,
But as only lungs can gather,
Is the depth of loss quick retrieved,
When breath catches up to a friend’s quickened return -
The eyes hear with love
The skin’s silent dance

So we merry tread
As if by appointment in some secret calendar
Mirth springs like clockwork
And while many are your rays,
Shadows brew no fewer,
And webs gather even in the busiest chambers:
Asking, which side are we on?
Now indebted, or in afterlife?

When Snows Melt

It is at the summer’s quickening
where bloom blazes through the icy clay
when light throws doubt upon shadows
that I gleam that wintry existence has no living for.

The hermetically sealed being may not expire
yet it is never fresh,
and work done during the cold season only to survive
has its purpose evaporated like the spring snow.

Yet why do I bind myself to this past hibernal state
when I should be leaping in welcome at the present renewal;
I find there is nothing to bring to life
and secretly yearn to be what is not.

And yet I cannot put the coat back on
and step through the streets in stern precipitation
when visions were ascetic yet tranquil—
now are but machine desires of a closed ecology.

Helpless to return to the frozen pendulum,
unable to turn time’s gaze towards eternity,
this unforgiving matrix of change and constancy
is unchanging in its alteration.

Actuality

What makes thought?
Are the brambles of anxiety
the stakes of depression
the airy bounces of joy
and the spurs of ambition
but its myriad forms?

Where does thought make its home?
In the mores of morality
the charts of stocks
the stats of exercise
or through the locking groove
in the eyes of another?

How does thought recognize itself?
When a delectable topic is subscribed
and its darling issues meandered about
and childlike vistas emerge flourishing
do the politics of fishes flurry charm
as combatant schools in the arena of attention?

When does thought think itself?
In abject despair
or witness to ecstasy
within or without
always overseas beyond its power
perennially descending.

What is the difference between actually thinking
and merely being mindful?
Often we are let to have our minds stick to some thing
an issue or topic, with little development.
What gives rise to development?
What brings forth actual thought?
What drives intelligence?

A misstep, a failure of charm
is thought that which is most shy
and most selective of all?
Or does thought rise at the absolute need
in a gathering storm of possibility
short-circuiting worlds in timely acts?

Perhaps thought is everything and nowhere
but no less eternally accompanied
by its navigator, the question
who stirs its flame
and lets show a way
whence there were none?

Lives of Knowing

Come as you are
and let your reach fall
into the palm of your mind -
there I will be waiting.

You see me as one
amidst your own life as many
drudging through the web of events
with a hungry sight of what is beyond.

Each moment of effort
initiates a passage of death
but through the weaving of habit
brings forth a new life.

Yet the horizon may grow dim
and my vision pale when, through
countless arcs you traverse the paths
snow trodden by the regularity of time.

As if two clocks were fighting to set the time
when all are its mere instruments;
your bones are its arms to foretell this now
as every being bears its mark.

And then another horizon rises,
shining less but shining still
yet your reach is shorter
and your sight bearing no hunger.

I will call to you through yourself,
though through echoes and reflections;
for I am ever on the surface
that is felt most deeply within.

And you will know the path must remain open
by whatever force and resource you can muster
and rally to my summons,
for those most trapped heed my call.

Then you arrive at the appointed time
through rain and hail, and heart-stopping frost
through soft lullabies of fatigue and thieving boredom
you arrive to punch in the clock.

At each of our gatherings I forget who you are
yet we pick up where we left off since you remember me
for I know everything but you
and you recall all that has been taught to you -
as if we were to speak tomorrow
your knowledge of things that only I could teach
bears witness to many lives lived drinking from the cup
sparing no drop for regret and less for fear -
for you did not first find me through theory or hearsay
but through courage self-forged on the temporal battlefield
when trapped you pushed, laughing bright in absolute darkening
and won my eternal glory.

Intermediary

What is a button but what is pressed with a finger
or a lever thrown by a hand
and yet my screen holds many such utilities
without anything ever being pressed or shifted.

Or, pressure was applied
and appearances implied something was pressed
yet I merely touched a piece of glass
which nonetheless held all my thought captive.

Is it that we bring something unnatural into the world,
or bring the world into our nature?
What touches makes
and is none better than the mind pristine?

What efficiency is it that spawns an industry
and renders itself more integral—
or perhaps that this brings to appearance
a world in the likes of our essence.

Yet for who are these tools?
Is there somebody prior to the skill?
Or does the equipment graft us upon it
whereupon the wielded welds the wielder.

The thought that rips and shreds

The Code of Life

There are no secrets
Only a dream.
Everything is in plain view, everything is
Given
It is not nature that loves to hide
But the mind that is not yet able
To deign the key

How many aeons has life attempted
To unlock the mystery of its being
Through perceptions azure and filled
It needed a life still further
A life beyond life—
Waking

But this new life begins steeped in frothing inclinations
Intoxicated with discovery and with its own standing far above anything
It soars towards reality’s sun
Ignoring time’s hallowed cry

Only fire can teach this new life
Only fire can etch into its being as memory
The forgetting of pain and the lessons of its failure
And only fire can breathe anew the beats of its wings
To fly still higher

Memories become keys
And keys form a chain
To unlock other keys
That always have been there;
For they were and are granting access, freely
To the double doors of the horizon.

We are so much older than we think
And so much younger than we imagine
We stand on a mountain of forms,
Each ceaselessly borrowing from the other
Importing, exporting, compiling and linking
Executing lavishly through sore designs:
Brewing a new mixture, a new language,
A new style and a new form; forever adjoined
With its parents to continue the story
That retold and foretold
The jaunt movement of this delight manifold.

It is when capacities reach their maturity
When the form is well worn and tested
That the time to descend begins
And go to the foot of the mountain
To the brooding source of its height
And contribute what one always stood under
That others may have something better to stand on.

The key of keys glimmers as a creed
It is logic that casts the mind’s veil
And nature’s expectations unlock
The code that makes sense of us and each other
Reaches through us from the bones of existence
Through the heart’s gut, to the tip of our senses,
Manifesting, manifesting:
There are no secrets
Only a dream.

To disallow wings to fly

As if breathed from the same lung

Sweetest reward the present’s lifespan

There was grown a stern devotion

Still were your every emotion

That quickened rises when you’re most forsaken

Virtue and vice each dance with time
But only one bears a gift for the future’s mime
While the other steals with the past’s calamity
A good custom repairs with balanced amity

Here is where our consciousness will most inhabit
Sprung freely from life’s love for habit

Age of Morpheus

A dream is the thought that wanders
never being prescribed to a deigned orbit
and less to a lawful act,
it reaps remnants after its own image
and shapes visions from a horizon
broken by desire’s infinite delight.

Everywhere a mirror stands ready
to summon your nether mind
and seed consumption of its darker reflection—
to find yourself in a million figures
is to bask in an ideal that is somehow
neither yours nor theirs.

But like clever stratagems of old
a screen’s first purpose is to conceal
to render less as many, and many less
and thence to elicit lethargy when the mind’s agent
most requires express wakefulness—
for intelligence is a work of net.

And so it seems most peculiar
that the network carried by light itself
in numbers of precise googols
should come to feed the voiceless thoughts
which hammer relentlessly at the mind’s attendance
for there is no dream without its sleeper.

We Can Fix Your Heart

We can take out your heart
and completely replace it with another’s
but we cannot take your mind
and replace it with another’s.

All the wonders of medicine
discovering our body as mere gears
to be oiled by drugs or outright supplanted
and become unburdened by this vessel.

But we cannot split your soul in two
sorting good from bad
to switch out the worse with better
and ease the weight you must bear.

Why can we not reach into you
like we do with chemicals and surgery
to repair what has been weakened and damaged
and restore you to your freedom?

What does the mind have that the heart does not?
What does the soul have that cannot be touched by drugs?
What is your spirit made of such others cannot enliven you
yet you must enliven yourself through their presence?

Is it history that sets them apart?
But a heart has its own scars and tears
that bear witness to times of intensity and strife
or just the iteration of a slow development.

Is it thought that holds the difference?
But thought cannot be anything else but that of mind
for it is what mind is and does
the being that feels its being.

Is it a quantum of dependency—
that the mind depends on much more,
but this would not explain, why
it could not be broken down.

Is it freedom, then, that decides
but a heart has some small measure of its own developing too
yet it does not know it—ah,
the consciousness that knows itself to be
can never lose this certainty,
tighter than a beat to its heart
does the mind breathe with its thoughts
as the soul gathers in the clearing of their horizon
and the spirit that looks back to delight in their company
recognizes its self-knowing spirits in their wool of history
or, as they say,
the cat is out of the bag.

We can take out your heart
and completely replace it with another’s
but we cannot take your mind
and replace it with another’s.

Service of Death

Enter the service of death
Whose harbingers echo in memory impersonal
The gold authority of Milton’s and Shakespeare’s
Whose letter thrusts like a lance from a foregone tourney
Balks the absurdity of their existence—
Which now, sweet effect, does beckon allure
To the present feeling, that unkempt reservoir
That bids the cup forgetfulness and unlearning
Our most sacred gift

But through the old phalanx of these words
A chance javelin is thrown at life’s charioteer most hasteful
And breaks their delicate wheel, in which turn
Tutelage is sworn at the falling upon the ground
A pain that transcends all wind grazed joys:
To learn to speak anew in their language
And abide in foreign laws make their names sacred
Molded is the soul now and forever
To reanimate their world in yours

Through these once wordy manacles
The brainy stem has grown many branches and leaves
Where each enjoys a sunlight to the other’s surplus
And communion gathers in corridors watery
Time’s crusty experience, that physical reimbursement,
Revealing finally the meaning of these old censors
Whose regal speech sang neither of words nor names
But reached only so far that your voice may have cause to sing;
And intone to heart, beneath the mind’s politicking:
That they have in all this time
In your service been

Come Back To Me

A crawling specter rises
moving constantly without going anywhere
the house is empty, nobody is home
a fallout enervating leeches the marrow of my mind
and I cannot remember why.

Going from one alienating place to another
wrecks mental decapitation upon the day
before the survival instinct has had time to kick in
I linger in this waiting room
unable to pay a debt I have not accrued.

Why did you do it?
Introspection asks, reclining on the couch
Why was it done to me?
I retort, shifting the scales
What is desire for? Is the question.

Fiery, flesh wakens flesh
only to bind the promethean giant
where the beak of pity daily carves at mind’s body
drawing directions to the future
and nightly the past grows together.

I seek the spirit that is lost to me,
but it is I who is lost to the spirit—
I am he who has become dead,
whose body life flees,
and attracts dust and fleas—
a hand enfolds within my own
stirring impulse, circular reaction
and I hear the whispers
I always thought my own
come back to me.

To Still Survive

Wrapped in a coat of ice
I pass through the doors of hell
wearing a stone mask
to shield my heart from mental storm.

All the senses to battle stations
while the mind conjures tactics
for only one destination can be computed
as each moment requires is own design.

It is not until after that the cost is known
and all the fatigue as avalanche buries
with icy thorns enveloping the spirit
and poisons the morrow through wanton vigilance.

When the light of my presence fails to reflect
my world loses its color
and I begin to believe
that I will never be free.

But though this self-imposed frost comes harsh
harsher is your silent spite and irradiating disdain
and so I give you my coldest countenance
in order to still survive.

Spite’s Unlife

There is not a world I can escape to
where I do not find your invidious presence
whose proximity reverses all light
and by vile suction begets freezing void.

And I harken to your oppressive fragrance
by redoubling it with my own,
to crush this crushing creature of preset time
which rudely abducts eternity’s future moments.

My mind is a throne of blades
melted together by hatred’s free fury
that keeps molding and shaping
every mental fiber to its doomed ire.

What delight to murder your image
and crucify your spirit
to turn that evil gaze upon itself
and see your soul break behind those dewy eyes.

But the more I heap upon this raging pyre
the further I deepen my own burn
and tattoo into memory this superficial spite—
becoming the very thing I despise.

Forgiveness’s sly falsetto beckons me to return
to see the other as oneself, as all of God’s prideful creatures
yet justice’s formal tenor calls to see it for what it is
and squash this misdeed that restoration may commence.

Despite all, whether by justice or mercy
this unlife persists and etches deeper into the bone
when sometimes one must be prescient in holding on to one’s own hunger
and let all this be
the bad dream that it is.

Horizon of the Sea

I love the ocean horizon not for its remoteness
But for its nearness
The line that stretches across from side to side
Keeps each raw moment to the same as the last
Affording easy entry, from left to right
And right to left

Unlike everything else in nature,
The ocean horizon mimics our own mathematical nature
The line that keeps steady when all else toils,
Rolls and recoils, spends its time thriftily avoiding its shatter-some feast
While the horizon stays fast,
In dividing simply

Unseen to the naked I does nature
From nature recharge the return of its leap
As the sea below and the sky above each are
At home in this mutual separation
As a judgment splintered comes back to its judger
Their sundered whole

Purgation on the Mount

When we find ourselves
On the shore of a vast endless past
Where the horizon calls for an infinite scroll
Buried in the seams that mind,
Then does the expanse’s shine convey
The mountain that stood waiting in life’s reflection
To welcome your step
To bid you begin

Its majesty is daunting
And where beauty is inspired
A bond soon weaves as tight as fate
But the mountain opens these as your friends;
Where its rugged skin reveals gentle paths
Elevation is curbed to a rising shoulder
And to those athirst it grants from its veins
Bittersweet waters flowing freely

Each step invites another
As easy as the wind grazes the ground
But ten become hundred and soon a thousand
And endurance begins its trying song,
Yet sweetly counterpoints the voice to stop
Soothing are its images that cease the ache
To bring that energetic turbulence
To a premature end

But a deeper promise resonates further still
The compact made with the mount
Signed by beauty terrestrial born
And witnessed by celestial mind;
Resolve shakes the limbs to steel their footing
And heart is taken to transport impulse
From heavenly industry
Into earthly art

Fragile is this manner of perseverance
That is fed solely by such sky-trod will
But its glad punctuation arrives in the will of the body
That unshakably grows trees of memories etched in movement,
Here a true third voice sings
Born by toil but found in beauty
And bears witness to its own epiphany
Sunlit in the arc of the valley below

As little as there is left to go
It could have been thrice the climb
As limbs now sing in rhythmic absolution
And courage impervious is dulled only by cold rest,
But zenith propounds no other purpose
For here a pool of water crystal clear coalesced
Baptizes the spirit in Eden’s finest garments
Dousing enflamed desire in sweetest satisfaction

Now your beginning has made its end
As the mountain throne yielded all its treasure
That sorely was fought under gravity’s pressure
Forging your all- confounding and conflicting voices
Into a single soul blade cut from the body’s hammer
Ensconcing hearth in motility’s furnace
To bring home into the eye’s view
From every peak

Time across distance peels our myriad souls
To their innermost soul
Waiting all this time to be known
To let who you are become -

Desync

The time we once had
becomes a timestamp for the rest of our lives,
for each year that comes around
and every birthday we celebrate
we remain connected though we are not,
but by some hidden law of existence
we march lockstep with time
and decay together.

This mark is matched in depth
only by its intensity,
and like ramessessian reliefs
they are not easily carved over
and come to stand most out
when truth’s sun blazes freely.

And though I draw secret delight
in reminiscing these etchings upon my bones,
this screen betrays against my outer desire—
and against its deigned purpose—
how my heart has been
frozen stuck in a loop
and how with all the play that our time represented
I am out of sync.

To Hit the Mark

A lifetime can pass
without the knowledge
to hit the mark
much less know that it is there.

If life is a game of snooker,
then which player has hid our color
and do our strikes count for more
than the fouls of our opponent?

How many attempts does it take?
How many attempts can one afford?
How far must one be cast adrift
when shooting past the target?

But does it matter how far beyond
one falls
when all it takes is to lift oneself
and turn
and put in another throw.

If the mark moves, it can be shot at
from any side
and if it stays still
you have all the advantage.

Sin can remain that which
is at the end of our distance to God
or it can be the distance whose cross
animates our deepest promise.

Fahmida

Fahmida bears little greeting
as she opens the door ajar,
while her eyes remain downcast
whether out of will or habit
she refuses to meet mine.

But an instant is all my heart needed
when sight devoured recklessly
to behold her beatific countenance
with the feeling of iambic justice
whose terror brought me to this threshold.

She asks me to be made clean
to which I acquiesce without reserve
in the softest tone I could muster
for when I step aside to next chamber
my soul is already awash with tremor.

She lies in nature’s garment upon my return
and makes neither gesture nor speech
but reclines in the naked truth
of certainty’s comfort
and awaits meekly for those athirst.

No blemish or mark caresses her skin
her form only holds the fullness of hours
and all is granted to those who require
to find fiery respite in an ancient song
and remember one’s story in the eternal midnight sky.

As particles falling to make an aurora
so imagination was enflamed
an electric curtain that swept across the body of her idea
for my thoughts firmly held her out of maniacal genius
as she conjured day out of the world’s night.

There is no sin in a world of love
but that world is not here
the world of measure and law rule our souls
and while their diligence mean well from eternity’s city
no prayer can steal into time’s garden without noise,
but an angel fell, not for our sins, but for our hearts
and remains disguised in the corners of our eyes
rising only when reason’s cunning
through rhythmic absolution
exposes wisdom’s laughter.

The Alchemists

The alchemist of old sought neither treasure nor glory
But the power to make these by command,
To discover the sacred formulation that could transmute
The base and worthless into the most valued high

The new alchemists fervently seek treasure and glory
But of the heart and of emotion
To turn their minds into the alembic
Powered by the fire of their soul

Both sought the same through different means
Yet the means are merely that of outside and inside
And so their distinction betrays a superficiality
At the core of the human being
Whose insides are outside
And outsides inside.

O’ many faced one, you whose countenance
No amount of masks can cover quickly enough
You dance through time with a hidden grace
Visible only to those who have survived

What are these many faces of your essence
Reflected as true distinctions in the taste of their existence,
That proceed forward to ground the weight of your conscious self
As it touches the reality of its being
To make its eternal wake

Evert category I dare imagine
Instantly falls short of your present potency—
The mind leaps beyond the speed of light—
And here falls that remainder that is me

Vessels embodied shed their tranquility for time’s vampirism
That a future may have a past, and a past a future
These dimensions are the permanent recoil
Of souls enclosed in branches of congealed conceptions
Ripe plucked as rhythmic fruit whose sight
Gifts the taste of desire

First is the immediacy of contact
Through sensuousness delightful
It enabled in the purity of force
The original language of being
That spoke of exertion and resistance
And of fatigue and overcoming
Of sumptuous energy and pushed power
And taught this verdant reality to precede all else
For the creature arrives bodied
And learns first the economy of these

Second is the mediation of distance
Through mental retreat bewildering
It activates the universal tethering that binds
All existence to a meditation on what was
A freezing logos appears beneath appearances
And throws to vanity all the sanctity of duty and courage
For here a reflected reality precedes every other
As the creature rises to godhood
And learns of its absolute powerlessness

Is it loss of the first or the gain of the second
That brings the creature most sadness and disdain?
Perhaps a loss is a gain and a gain a loss
And through both there is a deeper gain
And a further loss—
And how has this grown together thread
Not continued to weave
This tapestry of life
Whose watchword rings: continue?

Weighted by nothingness
And carried by being
The creature walks through two dimensions
Whose exposure is a moment and a breath
And the thread is asked to grow a great blanket of memory
To prepare for the coming of death’s winter
Whose length knows no end
In depths without recall.

Desire and death, companions of history
As they quest through the human heart
To unveil the marionettes puppeteered by their former selves
In chambers where no thought has traversed
For it arrives either too early or too late
And skips that most necessary beat
Where all the pieces fall together.

Is the human one or are they many?
Is this a question of time or of place?
A question of duration or context,
Of endurance or tranquility?
But leap by mental vertigo to think horizons vertically,
And see each share a simple difference:
From beginnings and endings,
Through perennial flow immobile circuit
To what takes or what provides -

Water, Life

Before you quenched my thirst
with your lips
and washed my face
in your words,
was life only the thing that survived
but was not lived for,
and while much was written
little was said.

When your eyes looked into mine,
and mine into yours,
did the double doors of the horizon unlock
and push aside the dusty scaffold
through a wind so great
that it held me in your embrace
where time was lured into eternity
and everything dissipated but your mind.

The water you give
contains more mystery than ancient scrolls
for all the worship of the sun
cannot but echo the light of your touch,
for wherever you grace my skin with yours
feelings from life’s first awakening

Elysium

Some things are meant to be easy
some things are supposed to just happen
some things need you to let go.

Whether it is about taking charge
or being the enjoyment of another’s
you live the decision they made.

Neither ought nor need
are adequate to this
that simply is.

There Is A Depth Within Me

There is a depth within me
I can never rewrite
Just as stars in the night-sky
I will never reach

And yet their light reaches me
All the same
And remain faithful
Revealed by the cover of darkness

But it is the sea, the forests
And mountains
That stay silent in this darkly display
When all else appear to flicker
And punctuate with glimmering delight

The windows of these huddled hearths
Like barnacles upon a Leviathan
Build their history steadfast on time’s sandy beach
Saved at each moment by mores unfathomable
As fragile as a melody’s path, their sense is but
A whisper through the doors of perception

But empty are these words
For they are mere echoes
Chanting but a forgetfulness of life—
Just as that light that travels from these stars
Whose source has long vanished

But not this moon
Who is our reminder
That day is but a day’s night away

Between Lost and Found

I don’t know when it first began
but I have been coming here to this place
in search for tranquility and peace
amidst homely surroundings.

A cabin in the woods,
a trail through the fields,
a flower atop the mountain,
a mirror in the river.

Furniture built from self-borne planks
keep my scavenged books in tidy memorabilia,
and on the table sit a pair of aviators
bearing witness to someone’s character.

I return here to find
what I didn’t know was missing
and by the time I leave
it is lost to me what I always wanted.

But while here, my mind is set to play
to read frivolously of lost worlds
go batting of embodied visions
and see them hurled across the splintered horizon.

Here is my zone
my innermost desires are fulfilled
nowhere else I want to be
lest they bring me closer to reality.

A world is a sly conditional
and secretly a perennial draft,
breech but one border
and this plainsong shatters.

Whether by accident or design
once the limit is observed, the shell has cracked
and the old rushes in to fill the new
unless truth arrives face to face.

Across the edges of my world I saw him
who was in my likeness exact
and pursued the things I would pursue
but he is wrong and must be stopped.

Intercepting his course, I threw the first punch
and brought war between his world and mine
even though mine was mangled
and held his in context.

Conflict stems from two zones
competing to be the world
each fighting for their idea’s survival
unwilling to become a moment of another.

But why should one world willingly submit
to be a zone of another’s, a mere view
when a world is made unbounded by truth
how then can certainty bind it?

He had his paraphernalia like my own
and no doubt had visions that stemmed from the same hunger
but he had not found what I had lost
for it was my loss that led to finding him.

Yet had I found or been found,
and if found, who would find me?
Did I lose or was I lost,
if lost, what could lose me?

Is conflict the crucible of this being’s memory
whose nether tendrils strive to bring to actuality
such that it may rehearse its own gesture
and behold in our furrowed faces its own.

In the struggle I defeat him,
or he defeats me,
he is part of my world or I am part of his,
either way, each of our own worlds are null—
for the world we share binds us to its truth
and the task becomes not to push one’s view
but to integrate that of another
just as you are joined to the world that is thought.

Cato the Elder

A new man emerges
forged from his love of virtue
and duty to his country
he lives with unyielding discipline.

Battling on the field as well as in courts
be it with words or swords,
against foreigners or countrymen
none are spared his righteous vigil.

Choosing his companion not out of desire or wealth
but for the alignment of noble values,
and upon this soil grew an unfaltering love
whose fruit was a son.

Deemed too important to be left to expert slaves
he educated him after his own mold
yet adjusted to the boy’s weaknesses and strength
and saw him grow to an accomplished citizen.

For who was this person
who could dedicate himself to virtue so,
without ever asking
what is it was?

What were you really loyal to, Cato
was it virtue or the beauty of its idea?
Or was it the importance it bestowed upon you
for pride never seemed to stand in the way of your virtue.

Where does virtue cease and oppression begin?
And where does efficiency end and virtue continue?
When, if ever, does virtue allow for vice? Unless it always contains it too some degree.

Why could you be compelled by virtue where others failed?
Why, if virtue makes one virtuous, would anyone fail to choose it?
For was there ever a creature that did not pursue the good
to the very best of its ability, always?

Or does light cast on the virtuous garment
reveal its own shadowy patches
in threads of pettiness and scorn
whose pestilence turns virtue’s service from the good to the self.

Yet how can virtue shine so strongly without its knowledge
for without reason it amounts at best to stoicism,
yet you are somehow better than this –
and worse;
for you always put your mind to action
yet let that action remain without thought
and so you reach the apex of conscience
but slip so easily into the nadir of morality;
and obtusely refusing to know this,
you persist gleefully in the pendulum
but this last commitment to stale betterment
tips the scales towards tragedy.

To feel better or bettering one’s feeling. Can you tell the difference? Can you live the difference?

The Ocean Mirror

As heavy as the seas are
With their swirling depths
And twisted currents
There is always a lightness on the surface
That can be cupped by a child’s hand

Malleable from top to bottom
Like a range of cells
Where each atop the other
Carries their weight as the body swirls

Life, A Story

A life is a span of a story
A story a beginning, middle and end
Each part has its start
And its end, which makes it like a story

And so a story are stories of stories
As life is a life of lives
Each with a beginning, middle and end
Weaved through itself with no end

I know I have to move on, but

I know I have to move on, but
the smile on your face returns joy
from every corner of the world
and I know that you smile for me no longer.

I know I have to move on, but
the goofiness of your ways makes my heart jump
beyond the clouds of metaphysics
but I know you do not become a clown for me.

I know I have to move on, I really do, but
the shine of your beauty and your artful eye
invite only divine appropriations
and I know that I am so very mortal.

I know I have to move on, but
the music of your memory brings my mind
to dance as if life was only beginning
but I know that I am only closer to the end.
